# Supply Chain Resilience and Sustainability dynamics under influence of simultaneous Climatic and Systemic disruptions

## Research objective
Investigate supply chain resilience (SCR) and sustainability (SCS) strategies dynamics under the influence of simultaneous climatic and systemic disruptions

## Methodology
Systematic literature review (SLR) to learn how research suggests strategies to manage SCR and SCS under combined uncertainties, their synergies and trade-offs.
Framework used: PRISMA 2020

## Research questions
- RQ1: What are the primary drivers, strategies, and conceptual frameworks for integrated sustainable-resilient supply chain reconfiguration identified in the literature?
    
- RQ2: What are the key synergies and trade-offs between sustainability and resilience objectives when reconfiguring supply chain networks?
    
- RQ3: How do reconfiguration strategies differ in addressing the distinct challenges posed by slow-burn climate change versus sudden systemic shocks, and what are the key identified research gaps for future inquiry?

## Key findings
- Lack of integrated models combining climate change and systemic disruptions — most studies treat them separately.
    
- Weak linkage between sustainability and resilience — limited work on their trade‑offs, synergies, and nonlinear transition effects.
    
- Methodological silos — dominance of static, deterministic models; shortage of dynamic, AI‑driven, or stochastic approaches.
    
- Geographical and sectoral bias — underrepresentation of Global South and resource‑intensive sectors.
    
- Missing human & organizational factors — leadership, governance, and multi‑tier coordination largely overlooked.

- Poor visibility into transition dynamics — little research on performance decline during restructuring

A key finding is that the transformational efforts in sustainability integration fail due to unpreparedness for the change. Sustainability transformations require defining measurable impacts, incentives and short-wins to support successful transformation. Hence there is a need for a hypothetical framework that considers sustainability adoption as a transformation project.

**Agile R-S Transformation Model (Agile RSTM)** 

- Theoretical model to link long‑term structural redesign with daily operational adaptation
- Manage compound volatility and the flatten the performance dip during R-S integration
- Ensures strategic, governance, and operational decisions to align and reinforce mutually
    
- Consist of 3 Layers inspired from the Best-fit papers:
	- Strategy ( Macro Layer ) – Long‑Term Network Design
    - Governance ( Meso Layer ) – Coordination & Decision Alignment
    - Operations ( Micro Layer ) – Daily Adaptive Decisions
    
- Uses Modular implementation inspired from Agile Principles:
    - Risk of a total operational failure
    - Learn from small-scale updates
    
- And is executed cyclically through a Detect – Isolate – Reconfigure Cycle ( DIR Cycle )

**Limitations of the Review**

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Small & Selective Sample Size|- Only 6 papers met the strict inclusion criteria, <br>    <br>- Limiting and potentially excluding relevant studies.|
|Database & Access Constraints|- Reliance on Scopus and Web of Science; <br>    <br>- 5 relevant full texts could not be accessed.|
|Subjectivity in Scoring|- Despite a rubric, evaluation involves researcher judgment, creating potential interpretive bias.|
|Methodological Heterogeneity|- Included studies use very different methods (AI, econometrics, case studies), reducing comparability.|
|Limited Generalizability|- Findings may not apply to all industries or supply chain types due to varied contexts.|
|Reporting Bias in Literature|- Studies often highlight positive outcomes and underreport failures or negative trade‑offs.|

## Summary of Research

- Conducted a Systematic Literature Review on Supply Chain Reconfiguration under Climate and Systemic disruptions
- Screened 220 studies, evaluated 24, and selected 6 core papers.
- Analyzed reconfiguration drivers, mechanisms, and sustainability–resilience interactions under compound disruptions.
- Identified the non-linear dynamic in performance in integration of resilience and sustainability.
- Identified key literature gaps: fragmented modeling, limited compound‑risk integration, and missing social dimensions. 
- Developed a hypothetical framework to flatten the performance dip during reconfiguration and manage compound disruptions.
- Discussed the limitations of the literature review
